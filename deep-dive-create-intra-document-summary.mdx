## What It Does

Summarizes content from other sections within the same document being drafted. Unlike Summarize from Source, which reads from your uploaded source files, this action reads from the document Artos is currently building and produces a summary based on what's already been generated.

## How It Works

This action runs after other sections of the document have been generated. It looks at the content Artos has already produced in other sections and creates a summary based on that content.

**Source is the document itself.** This action does not read from your uploaded source files. It reads from the draft document. This makes it ideal for sections that need to synthesize or recap what's been written elsewhere in the same document.

**Depends on other sections being generated first.** Because it summarizes content from other parts of the document, those sections need to have been generated before this action runs. Artos handles this sequencing automatically, sections that use this action are typically processed after the sections they reference.

**Output is a narrative summary.** The action produces prose, not tables. If you need a tabular summary of data from within the document, use Create Intra-Document Table instead.

## Example Instructions

| Weak instruction | Strong instruction |
| --- | --- |
| "Summarize the document" | "Create comprehensive summary including: study design and objectives, patient population, key efficacy findings (primary and secondary endpoints), safety overview, conclusions. Limit to 1–1.5 pages." |
| "Write an introduction" | "Summarize the key content from the efficacy and safety sections to create an introduction that orients the reader. Include the study objective, primary endpoint result, and overall safety conclusion." |
| "Create a conclusion" | "Synthesize the efficacy results, safety findings, and overall benefit-risk assessment from the relevant sections of this document. Present conclusions with supporting quantitative data." |

**What makes the strong instructions better:** They specify which sections to draw from, what elements to include, and how the summary should be structured. Since this action is reading from the document itself, being clear about _which_ parts of the document to reference is especially important.

## Configuration Pattern: Executive Summary / Synopsis

The Synopsis section of a CSR is the most common use case for this action. It summarizes the entire document into a standalone section.

| Component | Configuration |
| --- | --- |
| **Action** | Create Intra-Document Summary |
| **Action Instructions** | "Create comprehensive summary including: study design and objectives, patient population, key efficacy findings (primary and secondary endpoints), safety overview, conclusions. Limit to 1–1.5 pages." |
| **Custom Instructions** | "Section should stand alone. Reader should understand the study without reading the full document. Use quantitative data where available." |

## Document-Specific Examples

### Clinical Study Report (CSR)

| Section | Instruction |
| --- | --- |
| Synopsis | "Create a comprehensive standalone synopsis covering: study design and objectives, patient population and disposition, primary and secondary efficacy results (with p-values and confidence intervals), safety overview (SAEs, deaths, discontinuations due to AEs), and conclusions. Limit to 1–1.5 pages. The reader should understand the study without reading the full report." |
| Introduction | "Summarize the study background, rationale, and objectives from the document. Provide sufficient context for the reader to understand why the study was conducted and what it was designed to evaluate." |
| Discussion & Overall Conclusions | "Synthesize the efficacy and safety results from this document. Discuss the primary endpoint result in the context of the study objectives. Highlight clinically meaningful safety signals. Provide an overall benefit-risk assessment and state the study conclusions." |
| Overall Safety Summary | "Summarize the safety findings presented across all safety subsections in this document — adverse events, SAEs, deaths, laboratory findings, vital signs, and ECG results — into a cohesive safety narrative." |

### Investigator's Brochure (IB)

| Section | Instruction |
| --- | --- |
| Summary of Data & Guidance for the Investigator | "Synthesize the nonclinical and clinical findings from this IB into a summary that provides the investigator with the key information needed to conduct the study safely. Highlight monitoring requirements, dose-limiting toxicities, known risks, and any precautions. This section should stand alone as practical guidance." |
| Introduction / Overview | "Summarize the compound's development history, mechanism of action, and current development status based on the content generated in this document." |

### Nonclinical Overview (NCO)

| Section | Instruction |
| --- | --- |
| Overall Nonclinical Summary | "Synthesize the pharmacology, pharmacokinetic, and toxicology findings from this document into an integrated nonclinical summary. Highlight the key findings that support the proposed clinical program, note any safety signals, and discuss the adequacy of the nonclinical package." |
| Conclusions | "Summarize the overall nonclinical findings and their implications for clinical development. State the safety margins, identify any risks that require clinical monitoring, and confirm that the nonclinical program supports the proposed indication and dosing regimen." |

## When to Use This Action

**Use Create Intra-Document Summary when:**

- The section needs to summarize or synthesize content from elsewhere in the same document
- You're writing an executive summary, synopsis, introduction, or conclusion
- The content should reflect what's already been generated, not raw source data
- You want a narrative recap that ties together findings from multiple sections

**Use a different action when:**

- You need to summarize content from source files → use **Summarize from Source**
- You need a table (not narrative) based on document content → use **Create Intra-Document Table**
- You need to copy content from a source verbatim → use **Copy from Source**

## Tips

- This action works best when the sections it references have detailed, well-generated content. If the source sections are thin or incomplete, the summary will reflect that.
- Be explicit about which sections to draw from. "Summarize the efficacy and safety sections" is better than "Summarize the document" because it focuses the action on the right content.
- For synopses or executive summaries, the instruction "Section should stand alone" in Custom Instructions is a useful framing, it tells the system that a reader should be able to understand the study without the full document.
