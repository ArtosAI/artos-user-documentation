---
title: "Deep-Dive on Actions"
description: "Learn how to apply each of the actions in different documents or sections"
---

Actions are the specific operations Artos performs to populate a section, executed in sequence during document generation to build the section's content.

Each action is designed to do one thing well. The action handles the _how_ (the mechanics of copying, summarizing, populating a table, etc.), and your instruction handles the _what_ (what content to focus on, what to prioritize, what constraints to follow). This means you don't need to write elaborate prompts, you just need to tell the action what to look for and how to present it.

When Artos generates your template, it automatically assigns actions to each section based on what it finds in your Word template and example files. In most cases, the assignments are close to what you need. Configuration is about reviewing those assignments and adjusting where needed.
