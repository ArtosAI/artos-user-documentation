## What It Does

Inserts a patient narrative for an individual subject from a listings document. This action is designed specifically for Patient Narratives sections, where each subject who meets certain criteria needs their own narrative writeup.

## How It Works

This action reads a listings document (typically a subject-level data listing), identifies individual subjects, and generates a narrative for each one.

**Automatic repetition.** Unlike other actions where you need to enable Repeat Sections to handle multiple items, Generate from Listings automatically repeats for every unique subject ID in the listings document. You don't need to configure repetition manually, the action handles it.

**One narrative per subject.** For each unique subject ID, the action generates a standalone narrative based on the data available in the listing for that subject. The narratives follow the structure and emphasis defined in your instructions.

**Source is a listings document.** The action expects a structured listings document with subject-level data. This is different from other actions that work with narrative source documents like protocols or study reports.

## Example Instructions

| Weak instruction | Strong instruction |
| --- | --- |
| "Write patient narratives" | "For each subject, write a narrative including: demographics, medical history, treatment received, event description with onset and resolution dates, concomitant medications, outcome, and investigator's assessment of causality." |
| "Describe each patient" | "Generate a narrative for each subject. Include: reason for inclusion (SAE type), demographics (age, sex, race), relevant medical history, study treatment and dose, detailed event description with timeline, actions taken, outcome, and causality assessment. Present events chronologically." |

**What makes the strong instructions better:** They define the structure and content elements for each narrative. Since the action generates many narratives from the same instructions, the instruction acts as a template for what each narrative should cover.

## Configuration Pattern: Patient Narratives Section

| Component | Configuration |
| --- | --- |
| **Action** | Generate from Listings |
| **Action Instructions** | "For each subject, generate a narrative including: demographics, relevant medical history, study treatment details, event description with dates and timeline, concomitant medications, actions taken, outcome, and investigator causality assessment. Present events in chronological order." |
| **Custom Instructions** | "Each narrative should be a standalone paragraph. Use past tense throughout. Refer to subjects by subject number, not name. Include all relevant events for each subject even if multiple events occurred." |

## Document-Specific Examples

### Clinical Study Report (CSR)

| Section | Instruction |
| --- | --- |
| Patient Narratives — Deaths | "Generate a narrative for each subject who died during the study or within 30 days of last dose. Include: demographics, relevant medical history, study treatment details, timeline of events leading to death (including all AEs and SAEs), causality assessment, autopsy findings if available, and investigator's assessment. Present events in chronological order." |
| Patient Narratives — SAEs | "Generate a narrative for each subject who experienced a serious adverse event. Include: demographics (age, sex, race, weight), relevant medical history, study treatment (dose, duration, any dose modifications), SAE details (verbatim term, preferred term, onset date/study day, severity, seriousness criteria, relationship to study drug, action taken, treatment given, outcome, resolution date). Present events chronologically." |
| Patient Narratives — Discontinuations Due to AEs | "Generate a narrative for each subject who discontinued study treatment due to an adverse event. Include: demographics, treatment details, the AE leading to discontinuation, any other concurrent AEs, and outcome after discontinuation." |
| Individual Subject Data (Section 14) | "Generate a comprehensive narrative for each subject. Include: demographics and baseline characteristics, treatment exposure, all adverse events (with onset, severity, relationship, and outcome), concomitant medications, key efficacy assessments, protocol deviations, and disposition. Organize chronologically." |

### Patient Narratives (Standalone Document)

| Section | Instruction |
| --- | --- |
| Full Patient Narrative Package | "Generate a detailed narrative for each subject. Include: enrollment and demographics, medical history, prior and concomitant medications, study drug exposure (dose, duration, compliance, modifications), all adverse events in chronological order (with start/stop dates, severity, causality, treatment, outcome), key efficacy assessments, and final disposition. Each narrative should read as a complete clinical story." |
| Focused Narratives — Hepatic Events | "Generate a narrative for each subject who experienced hepatic adverse events (ALT or AST \>3x ULN, total bilirubin \>2x ULN, or Hy's law cases). Include: demographics, hepatic event details, timeline of liver function test results, concomitant hepatotoxic medications, medical history relevant to liver function, and outcome. Reference Hy's law criteria where applicable." |

## When to Use This Action

**Use Generate from Listings when:**

- You're creating Patient Narratives from subject-level listing data
- Each subject needs an individual narrative writeup
- The source data is a structured listings document with subject IDs

**Use a different action when:**

- You're summarizing study-level (not subject-level) data → use **Summarize from Source**
- You need to copy existing patient narratives from a source document → use **Copy from Source**
- You're summarizing nonclinical reports → use **Summarize NCR**

## Tips

- The quality of the narratives depends heavily on the quality of the listings document. Make sure the listing includes all the data elements you want in the narratives (demographics, event details, concomitant meds, outcomes, etc.).
- Since the action generates one narrative per subject ID, ensure your listing has clean, consistent subject identifiers.
- Your instruction acts as a template for all narratives. Write it as if you're describing the ideal narrative for any subject, the action will apply the same structure to each one.
- Review a sample of generated narratives rather than all of them initially. If the structure or content needs adjusting, refine the instruction and regenerate.
